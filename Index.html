CLIMATE CHANGE WE MUST TAKE ACTION NOW !

LOSING ICE

1.2 TRILLION

Since the mid-1990s, we've lost around 28 trillion tons of ice, with today's melt rate standing at 1.2 trillion tons a year.

CO2 LEVELS

421 PPM

The concentration of carbon dioxide (CO2)in our atmosphere is the highest in human history. Pre-industrial CO2 levels were around 280 parts per million(ppm).

PEOPLE IN HIGH VULNERABILITY

3.6 BILLION

As many as 3.6 billion people live in areas of high vulnerability to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods, heat waves, extreme weather events and sea-level rise.

RISING TEMPERATURES

1.19°C

The world is about 1.19°C (2.14°F) warmer than the pre-industrial era. It might seem small, but it means a significant increase in accumulated heat.

CLIMATE ACTION NOW !

TRANSITION TO CLEAN ENERGY

Shifting from fossil fuels to renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power is crucial to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate climate change.

REDUCE CARBON EMISSIONS

Countries, industries, and individuals must actively work to reduce their carbon footprint by adopting sustainable practices, promoting energy efficiency, and implementing stricter regulations.

PROTECT AND RESTORE ECOSYSTEMS

Preserving and restoring natural ecosystems like forests, wetlands, and mangroves is essential. They act as carbon sinks, absorbing CO2 from the atmosphere, and help maintain biodiversity and ecological balance.

SUSTAINABLE TRANSPORTATION

Promoting public transportation, electric vehicles, cycling, and walking can significantly reduce emissions from the transportation sector, which is a major contributor to climate change.
